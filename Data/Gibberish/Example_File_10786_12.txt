Threat to expropriate not an expropriation   Ultimately, the “nearest the claimant came to show” an expropriation was with respect to Acapulco’s mayor’s public statement that the concession agreement should be “eliminated”. However, the tribunal found that no action followed this political statement made “in the heat of public debate”; as such, it could not amount to an expropriation.   Readers will recall that in a couple of arbitrations involving Poland, tribunals later found that a threatened expropriation was not a treaty breach in itself, pending the enactment of expropriatory measures.  Although Waste Management’s claims all failed, each party was left to bear its own costs.  Waste Management was represented in both phases of the dispute by Baker &amp; Botts LLP; Mexico was represented by in-house counsel, and assisted by Thomas &amp; Partners.
